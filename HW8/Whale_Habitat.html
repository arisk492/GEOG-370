For this assignment, I looked into the habitat areas for the endangered North Atlantic Right Whale. My real-world question is: what fraction of the North Atlantic Right Whale northeastern U.S. foraging area (where they find food) remians unobstructed by high-traffic shipping routes?


To start off, I downloaded vector data with the locations of critical habitats for the North Atlantic Right Whale, as well as a dataset on shipping fairways for U.S. coasts.


Next, I cleaned the shipping fairways dataset to remove features marking non-shipping areas, and used the dissolve tool to combine the remaining features into one. This is the link to my dissolved shipping fairways layer. Then, I used the difference tool to subtract the combined area of the shipping fairways from the critical whale habitat areas. This resulted in the unobstructed northeastern foraging area layer, which also still includes the data from the southeastern calving area. The datasets I created are both in EPSG 102008, which what I used for the map
